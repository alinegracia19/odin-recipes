Indomie Nyemek

Description

Indomie Nyemek is a popular Indonesian "upgrade" to standard instant noodles. The word nyemek refers to the texture—it’s not a soup, but it’s not dry either. It has a thick, rich, concentrated sauce that clings to the noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1 or 2 packs of Indomie Mi Goreng
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Chilies (cabe rawit) as much you can handle
  • 1 or 2 eggs
  • Sliced mustard greens (caisim), cabbage, or a few sliced meatballs (bakso).
  • About 200–300ml (just enough to cover the noodles halfway).

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan. Sauté the garlic, shallots, and chilies until they smell amazing.
  2. Add Protein: If you have bakso or sausage, toss them in now. Then crack one egg into the pan and scramble it roughly.
  3. Pour in the water and bring it to a boil.
  4. Add the Indomie noodles. Do not drain the water. Let the noodles soften in the boiling water.
  5. While the noodles are cooking, add the Indomie seasoning packets (oil, powder, and sauce) directly into the water. Add your extra Kecap Manis and Saus Sambal now too.
  6. If you want it extra rich, crack a second egg over the noodles when they are halfway done. Stir gently so the egg thickens the remaining water into a sauce.
  7. Add your greens (caisim or cabbage). Stir-fry for 1 more minute until the sauce has reduced and thickened but is still "wet."
  8. Top with the fried onions (bawang goreng) from the Indomie packet.
